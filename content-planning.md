# La politique Française pour les nuls - Content Planning

## Project Vision
**Goal:** Create a non-biased, educational resource that helps people understand French politics deeply enough to make informed voting decisions.

**Target Audience:** People with basic political knowledge seeking deeper understanding  
**Scope:** Last 20-30 years of French politics + institutional foundations  
**Content Type:** Static, evergreen content (official sources)  
**Depth Level:** Medium (understand HOW the system works, not just WHAT)  
**Primary Use:** Learning & reference

## Important Constraints & Notes

### Bias Prevention
- Present what parties have actually done (legislative records, votes)
- Present their stated plans for 2027 (from official party programs)
- Avoid commentary or opinion; stick to facts
- For each section, include: "what they promised vs. what they did"

### 2027 Election Context
- This is a forward-looking section (includes campaign promises)
- Should be verifiable against official party platforms
- Clearly distinguish between historical facts and stated future plans

### Scope Boundaries
- **In scope:** French national politics, national elections, major parties, government institutions
- **Out of scope:** EU politics (except French participation), detailed municipal politics, international relations
- **Time focus:** Last 20-30 years (1996-2026) with institutional background
- **No real-time updates needed:** Static content; no daily news

### Complexity Awareness
- Explain the "why" behind things (why is French voting system designed this way?)
- Explain tensions/complexities (cohabitation, weak vs. strong presidents)
- Avoid oversimplification while keeping language accessible

## Content Gathering Strategy

### Roles
- **AI:** Drafts content based on known institutional/historical information
- **Human:** Reviews, verifies against official sources, approves before publishing

### Workflow Per Section
1. AI drafts content in structured JSON format
2. Every claim includes a **source link** (official sources only) or is flagged
3. Uncertain/unverifiable content is tagged `⚠️ NEEDS VERIFICATION` 
4. Human verifies flagged items against official sources
5. Approved content moves to "ready" status

### Content Format
- **Storage:** Structured JSON data files (ready to plug into the website)
- **Language:** French primary, English secondary (bilingual)
- **Pacing:** One section per conversation session (focused, thorough)

### Gathering Order (Foundation-First for Accuracy)
Priority order is based on what's hardest to bias (constitutionally defined facts first):

| Priority | Section | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Glossary** | Defines terms used everywhere else — baseline vocabulary |
| 2 | **Institutions & Government Structure** | Constitutional facts, hardest to distort |
| 3 | **Electoral System** | Legally defined rules — factual |
| 4 | **Timeline of Major Events** | Historical record — verifiable dates & facts |
| 5 | **Major Laws & Reforms** | Documented legislative record |
| 6 | **Political Parties** | Ideology + history + 2027 programs |
| 7 | **Key Politicians & Leaders** | Profiles, careers, controversies |
| 8 | **Current Government Composition** | Snapshot of present state |

### Flags & Tags Used in Content Files
- `⚠️ NEEDS VERIFICATION` — AI is uncertain; must verify against source
- `📎 SOURCE: [url]` — Official source link for the claim
- `🗓️ SNAPSHOT: [date]` — For time-sensitive content (e.g., 2027 programs)
- `🔀 CONTROVERSIAL` — Topic where multiple perspectives will be presented

## Editorial Decisions (Resolved)

- **Partisan websites:** Use party websites **only** for their 2027 programs/promises (their own words). Label clearly as "official party source."
- **Polls & predictions:** Excluded. Factual election results only.
- **Controversial positions:** Present **multiple perspectives** — each party's stated position alongside the main opposition argument. No editorial comment.
- **2027 content updates:** Treat as a **snapshot in time** — clearly date-stamped. No obligation to keep updating it after the election.
- **Scandals & controversies:** Include as **historical facts**, sourced from official records, court verdicts, or public inquiries. Only where documentation exists.
