# PRODUCT.md — La politique Française pour les nuls

## Register
brand

## Users
French citizens (and French-speaking people worldwide) who feel lost when politics comes up: first-time voters, people who never studied political science, adults who want to form their own opinion before an election. They're curious but overwhelmed by partisan media. They use their phone.

## Product Purpose
A non-biased civic education reference that explains how French politics actually works: the institutions, the electoral system, what each party has done, what they plan to do, and the vocabulary to follow along. Goal is to help someone decide who to vote for with their own eyes, not through a media lens.

## Brand Personality
- Trustworthy and calm — like a good encyclopedia, not a pundit
- Direct and clear — no jargon unless explained
- Respectful of the reader's intelligence — presents facts and multiple perspectives, never tells them what to think
- French without being cliché — civic, not folkloric

## Anti-References
- Any political party's own website (biased by definition)
- French government websites (intimidating, bureaucratic)
- Newspaper editorial pages (opinionated)
- Wikipedia (too dense, too neutral to a fault)
- Flashy political campaign design (tricolore everywhere, bold slogans)

## Strategic Design Principles
1. Neutrality is visual too: no party color should dominate the UI
2. Mobile-first — primary reading context is the phone (commute, waiting room)
3. Bilingual (French primary, English secondary) — don't bury the language toggle
4. Every "needs verification" item must be visually distinguished
5. Content hierarchy: understand the system → know the parties → compare programs → vote informed

## Accessibility
WCAG AA minimum. French is the primary language; lang attribute must be set correctly.

## Deployment
GitHub Pages — must build to static HTML. No server-side rendering required.
